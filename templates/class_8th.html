Class 8th

Apart from just academics, mental preparation is also a crucial factor that can help the students in scoring better.

What are the course contents of Class 8 Maths?


This is the last book of the upper primary series. Class 8 maths all chapter solutions is the bridge to class 9th where children will deal with more formal mathematics. The focus is to involve students in the classroom or outside in doing mathematics. Space has been provided for children to engage in reflection, create their own rules and problems based on problems/tasks solved and following their ideas logically. Class 8 maths all formulas are simple and systematic.

  • Class 8 Maths Chapter 1 Rational Numbers recalls all the operations of rational numbers studied in previous classes and explains them further in-depth.

  • 8 Class Maths Chapter 2 Linear Equations in one variable focuses on applications of equations. The level has been increased by giving variables on both sides of the equation and equations reducible to the linear form.

  • Class 8 Maths Chapter 3 talks about Understanding Quadrilaterals, it introduces a child to different types of polygons (parallelogram, rhombus, rectangle, square, kite) and their dependency upon angles and different angle properties.

  • 8 Class Maths Chapter 4 Practical Geometry guides with the construction of quadrilaterals. Further, the chapter

  • Class 8 Maths Chapter 5 Data Handling deals with frequency, probability, histogram, pie chart.

  • 8 Class Maths Chapter 6 Square and Square Roots teaches the child all the possible method of finding square and square roots of any number.

  • Class 8 Maths Chapter 7 Cube and Cube Roots teaches the child about the application and meaning of square and cube as powers, different patterns with perfect square and cube, a method to find square and cube of any number.

  • 8 Class Maths Chapter 8 detail a student about cost price, selling price, profit and loss, tax and discount, simple interest and compound interest, and their applications which a child can relate to their daily life in Comparing Quantities.

  • Class 8 Maths Chapter 9 Algebraic Expressions and Identities deals with the multiplication of polynomials and applying standard identities.

  • 8 Class Maths Chapter 10 Visualising solid shapes introduces a student to various new solid shapes along with a brief discussion of their properties.

  • Class 8 Maths Chapter 11 Mensuration is expanded from further classes as it now deals with the area, perimeter and volume of polygons, 3d shape (cube and cuboid) and cylinder.

  • 8 Class Maths Chapter 12 Exponents and Powers deals with the use of exponents in standard form and explains the Laws of exponents for both positive as well as negative numbers.

  • Class 8 Maths Chapter 13 briefs a student about how two quantities are linked with each other and changes in one can affect the other Directly or Inversely.

  • 8 Class Maths Chapter 14 Factorization deals with the meaning of factors and teaches different methods of division of polynomials and standard identities. The emphasis is laid on understanding the concepts and reasons and not memorizing the formulas or the proofs for the problems. The textbooks provide an opportunity for students to Visualize various 2D and 3d shapes and introduce them with Euler’s Formula.

  • An introduction to quadrants, coordinates, types of the graph has been provided in Class 8 Maths Chapter 15.

  • 8 Class Maths Chapter 16 Playing with Numbers recalls the divisibility rules studied in various classes and helps to make understanding in students about the general form of a number.

The main concern is that a child learns and begins to feel confident about mathematics. The focus is to introduce ideas in a way that is moving towards becoming formal, which will help them in 9th standard as well. Diagrams, point blurbs are used to reduce long explanations and provide knowledge in a simplified way.


What do we study in Class 8 Science?


The textbook is designed to connect science that students study in school with their everyday life. Throughout the textbook, activities are suggested to clarify the concepts, develop reasoning and presentation skills. Paheli and Boojho accompany the children on this journey of learning.The language of the book has been kept simple and easy to understand.

  • The textbook begins with the discussion of agricultural practices in Class 8 Science Chapter 1 Crop Production and Management.

  • 8 Class Science Chapter 2 is an introduction to microorganisms, their living conditions, uses, diseases caused by them.

  • Class 8 Science Chapter 3 tells about Synthetic Fibres and Plastics.

  • 8 Class Science Chapter 4 elaborate the concepts of Metals and Nonmetals. Further, it focuses on the chemical properties of the material and its reactivity.

  • Class 8 Science Chapter 5 is about Natural Resources i.e. Coal and Petroleum and Natural Gas.

  • 8 Class Science Chapter 6 focuses on the process of combustion, the structure of flame and the efficiency of fuel.

  • Class 8 Science Chapter 7 is about the Conservation of Plants and Animals.

  • Class 8 Science Chapter 8 of the textbook is an introduction to the structure. Also, the functions of the basic unit of life- Cell.

  • 8 Class Science Chapter 9 explains the life process of reproduction in animals.

  • Class 8 Science Chapter 10 explains the age of adolescence and puberty.

  • 8 Class Science Chapter 11 deeply discusses the concepts of Force and Pressure.

  • Class 8 Science Chapter 12 draws its attention towards friction, its causes and the effects associated with it.

  • 8 Class Science Chapter 13 focuses on sound, its causes and the effects associated with it.

  • Class 8 Science Chapter 14 focuses on the Chemical Effects of the Electric Current.

  • 8 Class Science Chapter 15 is about Some Natural Phenomena like lightning.

  • Class 8 Science Chapter 16 will teach the students about what is light. Also, how it is produced, how images are formed and how our eyes function.

  • 8 Class Science Chapter 17 discusses the Solar System.

  • And lastly, Class 8 Science Chapter 18 of the textbook focuses on the increasing threat of Air and Water Pollution.

Resources and Development (geography)
What are the course contents of Class 8 SST Geography?


  • Class 8 SST Geography chapter 1 Resources is an introduction to what are resources, their types and need and methods to conserve them.

  • 8 Class SST Geography Chapter 2 gives a brief introduction to a few natural resources. It tells about land use, soil formation, problems of water availability, distribution of Natural Vegetation. Finally, it will discuss ways to conserve all the resources.

  • Class 8 SST Geography Chapter 3 Minerals and Power Resources explains the minerals, their distribution in different continents and India. Furthermore, the use of minerals and conventional and non-conventional resources of energy.

  • 8 Class SST Geography Chapter 4 Agriculture explains types of farming. Also, the importance of farms of the USA and India and development in the agricultural sector.

  • Class 8 SST Geography Chapter 5 focuses on the classification of industries, action, industrial regions. Lastly, it will discuss the distribution of major industries.

  • 8 Class SST Geography Chapter 6 focuses on humans as a resource. It tells about the population distribution, factors responsible and patterns of population change and composition.

The stories, case studies and pictures used in all the books make it engaging for a child. Also, it will encourage them to get involved in peer discussion and present their viewpoint on any respective topic. Furthermore, the language used is easy for a child to read and understand.

What is the course curriculum of Class 8 English main textbook and supplementary reader?


In the English ncert class 8 English, there are chapters and poems from the main textbook (Honeydew) and supplementary reader (It so happened). Also, they are curated by the best teachers to help the students score more marks in their examination. Class 8 English all chapter solutions are discussed in a comprehensive way for better results.

NCERT Class 8 ENGLISH MAIN TEXTBOOK “HONEYDEW”
(PROSE)

  • Class 8 English Chapter 1 “the best Christmas present in the world” by Michael Morpurgo is a story in which a letter touches the heart. It was a Christmas present from Connie.

  • 8 Class English Chapter 2 “The Tsunami '' is a tale of how Tsunami’s waves cause serious destruction through earthquakes. Also, there was a volcanic eruption that swept away so many family members.

  • Class 8 English Chapter 3 “glimpses of the past” depicts the story from the Indian past. Here, the great war of independence against British rule and stories are explained.

  • 8 Class English Chapter 4 “Bepin Choudhury’s Lapse of memory” is a funny story. Here, a person is tricked by his friend and avoided by him in the times he needs him the most.

  • Class 8 English Chapter 5 “The summit within” is a high spirited story in which the author shares his experience and feelings. Further, he was standing on the top of Mount Everest while doing so.

  • 8 Class English Chapter 6 “This is Jody’s Fawn” is a tale that transfers a message of compassion of love. Moreover, he considers both as a necessity of life without which human beings cannot survive.

  • Class 8 English Chapter 7 “A Visit to Cambridge” is a highly inspirational tale where two differently-abled people. In this, they share their experiences in a very pleasant way.

  • 8 Class English Chapter 8 “A short monsoon Diary” by Ruskin Bond about the silent miracles of nature and joy. All this is provided by the monsoon season. The author wants everyone to observe each thing with interest.

  • Class 8 English Chapter 9 “The Great stone face 1” is a popular shape that reminds the people about some spoken prophecy. The prophecy once persisted in that valley.

  • 8 Class English Chapter 10 “The Great stone face 2” is the continuation of the first part itself. Also, it is the personified text of part 1. Here, everything discussed in this is beyond imagination.

NCERT CLASS 8 ENGLISH MAIN TEXTBOOK “HONEYDEW”
(POEMS)

  • Class 8 English poem “The ant and the cricket” wanted to convey a very realistic message to its readers. Accordingly, we all are responsible for our future. And, will suffer if we ignore its aspects

  • 8 Class English poem “Geography lesson” is about the poet’s experience. Following, he views his place from so many feet above the ground and sees that the city is not looking organised.

  • Class 8 English poem “Macavity- The mystery cat” by the great poet T.S. Eliot is the story of mastermind Macavity. At this time, the cat commits crimes and leaves no evidence behind.

  • 8 Class English poem “The last bargain” is where a person is in search of a job. A job where he can be his own master and not in control of anyone.

  • Class 8 English poem “The School Boy” by William Blake is about a schoolboy who seems very unhappy. He wants to escape from his classroom and focuses on the downsides of learning.

  • 8 Class English poem “The Duck and the Kangaroo” is about the longing of the Duck to go beyond the pond. Especially, to explore the beauty of the world just like kangaroos can.

  • Class 8 English poem “When I set out for Lyonnesse” is a poem where the poet praises a place named “Lyonnesse”. Clearly, its culture has brought so many changes in the life of the poet in just a single visit.

  • 8 Class English poem “On the grasshopper and the Cricket'' is a nature poem. Hence, it is the description of hot summer and cold winter with a joyous mood.

NCERT CLASS 8 ENGLISH SUPPLEMENTARY READER
“IT SO HAPPENED”

  • Class 8 English supplementary reader chapter 1 “How the camel got his hump” by Rudyard Kipling is just an imaginative fable about a lazy camel who keeps skipping the work.

  • 8 Class English supplementary reader chapter 2 “Children at work” shows the sad life of children who have to become rag pickers for the sake of their families instead of studying.

  • Class 8 English supplementary reader chapter 3 “The selfish giant” is a fantasy tale about a giant who builds a huge wall to keep the little kids out of his garden. Accordingly, this is done so that they cannot play.

  • 8 Class English supplementary reader chapter 4 “The treasure within” is a hidden tale that tells that every student has his style of learning and interest. Hence, he may not be a topper but can be an achiever.

  • Class 8 English supplementary reader chapter 5 “Princess September” is a story about a young princess named September who has gifted a parrot inside a golden cage who dies.

  • 8 Class English supplementary reader chapter 6 “The Fight” is a conflict between two boys regarding swimming. Both the boys beat each other and became good friends by the end of the poem. Finally, they convey a message of love over anything else.

  • Class 8 English supplementary reader chapter 7 “The open window” is entirely based on an open window and focuses on appearance Vs reality and disbelief.

  • 8 Class English supplementary reader chapter 8 “Jalebis” is a moral based story that we cannot blindly depend on God for anything that our heart desires. We should work hard to make dreams come true.

  • 8 Class English supplementary reader chapter 9 “The Comet 1” is an astronomical text about an ambitious astronomer “Manoj Duttada”.

  • Class 8 English supplementary reader chapter 10 “The Comet 2” is the continuation of “The Comet 1” and is the conference of many leading scientists of the world.

Summary of Class 8 English all chapters are prepared with so much research which will be providing a good scope of learning to the students along with good marks.
