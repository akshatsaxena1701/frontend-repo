Class 11th (Science)

The syllabus of CBSE Class 11 is considered as a foundation for CBSE Class 12 board exams. The stronger the foundation, the better will be the performance in the CBSE board examinations. After having studied diverse subjects throughout your school life, now in CBSE class 11 Science, the real efforts begin. Though students are familiar with Physics, Chemistry and Biology in the earlier classes, still they face difficulties in adapting to the new curriculum.
Class 11 Science is more interesting and challenging than class 12. To overcome this challenge, proper guidance along with Individual classes are required for maintaining stability and mastering all the concepts for CBSE Class 12 Boards. Apart from that, the syllabus of JEE and NEET entrance examinations are entirely based on the concepts of class 11 and class 12. Remaining focused is the key to success for good performance in all exams.
Our online study material will be designed according to the individual learning requirements of a student. Ekal Shiksha proves to be the leading ed-tech platform for enhancing a student’s capability by conducting regular assessments without any hassle.

The textbook contains fifteen chapters in two parts. Part I contains first eight chapters while Part II contains next seven chapters. Physics is a subject whose concept will be used in every field be it social science, finance, administration, medicine, etc. the focus of textbook is on understanding rather than memorising. At the end of every chapter summary and points to ponder are provided to make it easy to overview the chapters. The textbook encourages the idea of seminars and mutual interactions.


  • Class 11 Physics Chapter 1 “Physical World” is an introduction to what is physics?

  • Class 11 Physics Chapter 2 “Units and Measurements” teaches about the international system of units, different dimensions, accuracy, precision, etc.

  • Class 11 Physics Chapter 3 “Motion in a Straight line” discusses position, path length, displacement, etc.

  • Class 11 Physics Chapter 4 “Motion in a Plane” discusses scaler and vector quantity and motion in a plane.

  • Class 11 Physics Chapter 5 “Laws of motion” is about an introduction to laws of motion, the law of inertia, and the concept of momentum.

  • Class 11 Physics Chapter 6 “Work, Energy, and Power” deals with the concepts of kinetic energy, potential energy and helps to relate such concepts with daily life.

  • Class 11 Physics Chapter 7 “System of Particles and the Rotational Motion”

  • Class 11 Physics Chapter 8 “Gravitation and various laws” effects related to it.

  • Class 11 Physics Chapter 9 takes the attention of students towards “Mechanical properties of solid”

  • Class 11 Physics Chapter 10 is all about the “Mechanical properties of fluids”.

  • Class 11 Physics Chapter 11 discusses the “Thermal properties of matter”.

  • Class 11 Physics Chapter 12 the child learns about “Thermodynamics”, laws of thermodynamics.

  • Class 11 Physics Chapter 13 “Kinetic Theory” and the behavior of gases.

  • Class 11 Physics In the last 2 chapters the detailed knowledge is provided about “Oscillations and the waves”.

Necessary tools and database in the form of Appendices are given at the end of the book. There are also some other appendices at the end of some chapters giving additional information or applications of matter discussed in them.


The book is divided into 2 parts. 1st part consists of seven chapters


  • Class 11 Chemistry Chapter 1 “Some basic concepts of chemistry”. It discusses the importance of chemistry, matter, different laws of chemical combinations, atomic theory, etc.

  • Class 11 Chemistry Chapter 2 “Structure of Atom” gives detailed information about The Structure of Atom and the different models associated with it.

  • Class 11 Chemistry Chapter 3 “Classification of elements and Periodicity” deals with the study of electronic configuration, nomenclature, and laws of periodicity of elements. It gives detailed information about the periodic table.

  • Class 11 Chemistry Chapter 4 “Chemical Bonding and Molecular Structure” discusses valence electrons, Ionic bonds, etc.

  • Class 11 Chemistry Chapter 5 “States of Matter” their intermolecular forces, thermal interactions, etc.

  • Class 11 Chemistry Chapter 6 “Thermodynamics” about the law of thermodynamics and other concepts like specific heat.

  • Class 11 Chemistry Chapter 7 “Equilibrium” its laws, factors, applications, etc.

The 2nd part consists of next seven chapters.

  • Class 11 Chemistry Chapter 8 “Redox Reactions”, the meaning of reduction and oxidation.

  • Class 11 Chemistry Chapter 9 “Hydrogen” discusses the position of hydrogen, occurrence, and isotopes, explains its preparation, properties, reactivity.

  • Class 11 Chemistry Chapters 10 focus on the “S-Block” of the periodic table.

  • Class 11 Chemistry Chapter 11 focuses on the “P-Block” of the periodic table.

  • Class 11 Chemistry 2 chapters are all about “Organic Chemistry”. It deals with the study of organic compounds.

  • Class 11 Chemistry Chapter 14 “Environmental Chemistry”, deals with the causes and cures of the major environmental issue: Pollution.

Geography – The Earth Our Habitat


In chapter 1 The Earth in Our Solar System a child learns about the sun, planets, and some celestial bodies that form the solar system. In chapter 2 the child becomes familiar with the Globe latitudes and longitudes. In chapter 3 meaning of rotation and revolution is give and the child learns about Motions of Earth. Chapter 4 describes about the need and use of maps. In chapter 5 The Domains of Earth and in Chapter 6 The Landforms of Earth are explained. Chapter 7 and 8 focus on location, climate, vegetation and wildlife of India.

History -Our Past


I Chapter 1 answers the questions of What, where, how and when about the past. In chapter 2 evolution of humans in explained from being hunters and gatherers to growing food on their own. The chapter 3 informs about the establishment of the Earliest Cities. Chapter 4 talks about an important source of history The Books and the Burials which helps us to know more about the past. The next chapter tells about the Kingdoms, Kings, and an Early Republic. It explains about the administrative system in past. Chapter 6 concentrates on the new questions and the ideas that prevailed in the past related to religion, language etc. chapter 7 is based on the story of a Great Emperor Ashoka and his Empire. Chapter focuses on establishment of Vital Villages and Thriving Towns. The next chapter discusses about establishment of trade, traders the kings and the pilgrims. Chapter 10 and 11 are based upon the New Empires and Kingdoms and the buildings, paintings and books of that period. All the chapters contain a short text, story line which will a help child to relate all the concepts of past with the present time. It will help them to understand how things were in past and how they have changed now.

Social and Political Life


I The chapter 1 and 2 introduces the child the meaning and concept of Diversity and the Discrimination people have to face. Chapter 3 and 4 focuses on the meaning of Government and the key elements of Democratic Government. Chapter 5 is based on Panchayati Raj. In further chapters the child learns about the differences in livelihood and administration of Rural and Urban Areas


The textbook contains twenty-two chapters which are grouped under five thematic units. Each unit has a brief write up and biographical sketch of a prominent scientist preceding the unit highlighting the essence of the chapters to follow under that unit. Each chapter has, on the first page, a detailed table of contents giving sub-headings within the chapter.


The textbook consists of 16 chapters. Theme of each chapter is introduced with examples from daily life and using the activities which students can perform. The textbook follows the constructive approach and provides opportunities to the students to construct knowledge themselves and explore and think beyond the syllabus.

Unit-1
1st unit Diversity in the Living World consist of four chapters. In this unit the child will get a description, including classification, of animals and plants from a taxonomist’s perspective.

Unit-2
2nd unit STRUCTURAL ORGANISATION IN PLANTS AND ANIMALS consist of next three chapters. In the following chapters of this unit, the structural organisation of plants and animals, including the structural basis of physiological or behavioural phenomena, is described.

Unit-3
The 3rd unit CELL: STRUCTURE AND FUNCTIONS consist of 3 chapters in which a description of cell structure and cell growth by division is given. A brief description about Biomolecules is also provided.

Unit-4
The 4th unit PLANT PHYSIOLOGY consists of 5 chapters. Description of physiological processes, in flowering plants, is given in the chapters in this unit. The processes of mineral nutrition of plants, photosynthesis, transport, respiration and ultimately plant growth and development are described in molecular terms but in the context of cellular activities and even at organism level. Wherever appropriate, the relation of the physiological processes to environment is also discussed.

At the end of each chapter a brief summary is provided. The emphasis has been given on the narrative style, illustrations, activity exercises, clarity of expression, coverage of topics.
