Class 11th (Humanities)

Many students pursue arts with an assumption in mind that it is the easiest among the three streams. However, this is far from the truth. You might see yourself as a troublemaker if you don’t put your best into this stream as well. Humanities offer the student exposure to the core subjects such as History, Economics, Political Science and Geography. All of these subjects are based on theoretical learning and require a lot of memory to retain. Humanities bring out a pool of opportunity for every individual.
There is a wide range of government jobs opportunities for Humanities students in the field of jurisdiction, diplomacy, and administration. Students need to be kept highly motivated and optimistic to perform better in their Class 11 Humanities. Students must be guided under the mentorship of someone who can keep a close check on their performance and teach them to present their answers in a presentable way to ensure good marks.
We at Ekal Shiksha, aim at ensuring that a student puts his best foot forward for subjects that require theoretical knowledge by providing them relevant knowledge by the experts. Our experts value quality over quantity and make you work harder while realising your potential.

Mathematics play important role in day to day life. It helps in developing logical reasoning, abstract thinking and imagination. The textbook helps in developing a thinking to understand the arguments and capacity to make interrelations among the concepts. The beginning of mathematics in 6th standard is initiated by testing the previous knowledge of child about numbers, their multiplication, division, number names, patterns, etc. It introduces to difference between digits and the numbers, place value system. Further the understanding of the child is developed using different measuring units. Process and applications of Estimation has been described to a student in such a way that they can relate it to daily life examples. Properties of whole numbers helps students to understand the mathematical operations in a better way. Divisibility tests and concept of HCF and LCM is developed in a better way. The book also introduces the basic knowledge about angles, lines and how a shape depends upon these factors. Symmetry is also included recalling the previous knowledge. Knowledge about negative numbers have been initiated in 6th standard which is further developed with fractions and decimals where the child learns about what are different forms of numbers and how to deal with them. Area and Perimeter is explained with the help of things surrounding the child’s life. Differences in basic concepts like fraction and ratio are explained. The textbook guides teachers as well as students to understand the concept, reason behind the process rather than just memorizing it. Many opportunities are provided in textbook where child will be verifying principles or patterns

Geography – The Earth Our Habitat


In chapter 1 The Earth in Our Solar System a child learns about the sun, planets, and some celestial bodies that form the solar system. In chapter 2 the child becomes familiar with the Globe latitudes and longitudes. In chapter 3 meaning of rotation and revolution is give and the child learns about Motions of Earth. Chapter 4 describes about the need and use of maps. In chapter 5 The Domains of Earth and in Chapter 6 The Landforms of Earth are explained. Chapter 7 and 8 focus on location, climate, vegetation and wildlife of India.

History -Our Past


I Chapter 1 answers the questions of What, where, how and when about the past. In chapter 2 evolution of humans in explained from being hunters and gatherers to growing food on their own. The chapter 3 informs about the establishment of the Earliest Cities. Chapter 4 talks about an important source of history The Books and the Burials which helps us to know more about the past. The next chapter tells about the Kingdoms, Kings, and an Early Republic. It explains about the administrative system in past. Chapter 6 concentrates on the new questions and the ideas that prevailed in the past related to religion, language etc. chapter 7 is based on the story of a Great Emperor Ashoka and his Empire. Chapter focuses on establishment of Vital Villages and Thriving Towns. The next chapter discusses about establishment of trade, traders the kings and the pilgrims. Chapter 10 and 11 are based upon the New Empires and Kingdoms and the buildings, paintings and books of that period. All the chapters contain a short text, story line which will a help child to relate all the concepts of past with the present time. It will help them to understand how things were in past and how they have changed now.

Social and Political Life


I The chapter 1 and 2 introduces the child the meaning and concept of Diversity and the Discrimination people have to face. Chapter 3 and 4 focuses on the meaning of Government and the key elements of Democratic Government. Chapter 5 is based on Panchayati Raj. In further chapters the child learns about the differences in livelihood and administration of Rural and Urban Areas
