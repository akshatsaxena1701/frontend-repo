What is the syllabus of Class 7 Mathematics?

The focus of the class 7 maths book is on making a child mathematise. The aim of mathematics is not merely being able to do quantitative calculations but to develop a child’s ability. By this, they can redefine their relationship with the world. In the 7th standard initiation of movement from concrete models, and experiences towards abstraction begins.

In class 6th attempt to introduce a child to the abstract nature of mathematics has been done. Thus, the 7 class textbook continues the spirit and uses the language which is easy for a child to read and understand.

  • Class 7 Maths Chapter 1 focuses on elaborating the “Integers” for the previous standard it introduces properties of addition, subtraction, multiplication and division of integers, making a child familiar with the application and function of both negative and positive integers. Also, word problems in the form of a story have been included which will engage the child with the text.

  • In 7 class Maths Chapter 2, a child at this time also learns multiplication and division of fractions and decimals.

  • In class 7 Maths Chapter 3, Knowledge of various concepts like average, arithmetic means, median, mode, uses of graphs, and an introduction to probability has been given to the student in the chapter Data Handling.

  • 7 class Maths Chapter 4 focuses on finding the solution to an equation and forming an equation from a solution and their uses in practical life have been taught to students, using Simple Equations.

  • In class 7 Maths Chapter 5, the concepts of geometry have been developed in chapters like Lines and Angles. Thus, a child learns about different types of angles and their properties.

  • In 7 class Maths Chapter 6, the triangle and its properties teach about various components of triangles like altitude, medians, angle sum property, exterior angle property and different types of triangles.

  • In Class 7 Maths Chapter 7 the concept of congruence of triangles is introduced to the child for the first time. Also, It explains the meaning of congruence and its criterion using games or relatable examples.

  • In 7 class Maths Chapter 8, the introduction of comparing quantities and percentages has been explained. Similarly, a child can use it in calculating and comparing their marks, dealing in the market etc.

  • In Class 7 Maths Chapter 9, the concept of Rational numbers has been extended from the previous class. Most importantly, operations of rational numbers, negative rational numbers are explained well.

  • In 7 class Maths Chapter 10, Practical geometry brings interesting activities along with. Consequently, it also allows the child to construct angles, triangles themselves using a compass.

  • Class 7 Maths Chapter 11 recalls from square and rectangle the chapter Perimeter and Area and moves to new topics like a parallelogram, circle. Moreover, It includes practical uses of the area and perimeter like in fields which helps a child to understand it better.

  • 7 class Maths Chapter 12 explains how to convert from one unit to another. Also, the Meaning of algebra, coefficient, introduction to monomial, binomial etc has been provided. Also, the addition of algebraic expressions and finding their solutions has been explained in chapter Algebraic Expressions.

What is the course curriculum of class 7 science?

The Class 7 Science book is designed in a way that it becomes more interactive and child can recall their own experiences and build concepts around them. Therefore, simple activities are included which can be performed easily by students and the language has been kept as simple as possible. Along with the two friends Paheli and Boojho, other pictures, illustrations, and cartoons make it very engaging for students.

  • Class 7 Science Chapter 1 talks about Nutrition in Plants and how they are derived.

  • 7 class Science Chapter 2 talked about Nutrition in Animals and how they are transferred to one another in an economy.

  • Class 7 Science Chapter 3 describes the process and sources of different fibres and how the fabric is made out of fibres.

  • 7 class Science Chapter 4 explains to the students What is heat, how is it measured and the methods of transfer of heat.

  • In class 7 Science Chapter 5, the child gets a brief about what Acids, Bases and Salts are.

  • In 7 class Science Chapter 6 the child will understand the concept of Physical and Chemical Changes.

  • Class 7 Science Chapter 7 tells the difference between Weather and Climate and the Adaptations of animals to climate.

  • 7 class Science Chapter 8 discusses Winds, Storms and Cyclones and their effect on the atmosphere.

  • Class 7 Science Chapter 9 tells about Soil Profile, preparation, properties and types of soil.

  • 7 class Science Chapter 10 tells the difference between the process of Respiration and Breathing and how organisms breathe.

  • In Class 7 Science Chapter 11, Transportation in Plants and Animals the child will learn about the circulatory system, excretory system and the transportation of substances in plants.

  • 7 class Science Chapter 12 is based upon the Reproduction in Plants. Motion and Time gives a brief idea. Also, it gives a picture of what is motion, how can one measure speed and time. Lastly, it discusses their unit and their dependency upon each other.

  • Class 7 Science Chapter 13 Motion and Time describes the different kinds of motions.

  • 7 class Science Chapter 14 is about Electric Current and its Effects.

  • Class 7 Science Chapter 15 introduces the child to the applications of the plane and spherical mirrors, and the way light travels

  • 7 class Science Chapter 16 is based on water as a precious resource. Further, it also describes the way to prevent the excess usage of water to sustain.

  • Class 7 Science Chapter 17 focuses on Forest and marks their essence in the lives of people.

  • 7 class Science Chapter 18 tells everything related to pollutants, wastewater plants, sewage etc.

Class 7 science all chapters are based on Facts, anecdotes and stories to make it more engaging and interesting for students.

What does a student study in class 7 SST?

The Class 7 SST history textbook focuses on the fact that the children’s life at school must link to their life outside the school. Also, it encourages children to reflect on their learning and to pursue imaginative questions and activities.

7 CLASS HISTORY (Our Past II) -

In context with history, the textbook focuses on telling our past.

  • Class 7 SST History Chapter 1 starts with Tracing Changes Through a Thousand Years that talks about new and old terminologies. Further, it talks about historians and their sources, old and new regions.

  • 7 class SST History Chapter 2 talks about New Kings and Kingdoms where we discuss the emergence of New Dynasties.

  • 7 class SST History Chapter 3 focuses on The Delhi Sultans.

  • Class 7 SST History Chapter 4 discusses The Mughal Empire. Specifically, It discusses Mughals, their traditions, successions etc.

  • The succeeding 7 class SST History Chapter 5 tells us about the history of various buildings, monuments. Also, this is what that we see or visit that is named Rulers and Buildings.

  • In Class 7 SST History Chapter 6, we get to know about the Towns, Traders and Craftspersons.

  • 7 class SST History Chapter 7 tells about the lives of Tribes, Nomads and the people living in settled communities.

  • Class 7 SST History Chapter 8 Devotional Paths to Divine talks about the Idea of Supreme God. Also, about various religious beliefs prevailing in the past.

  • 7 class SST History Chapter 9 discusses the making of Regional Cultures.

  • Lastly, Class 7 SST History Chapter 10 tells about the Political Formations of the 18th century.

7 CLASS GEOGRAPHY (Our Environment) -

  • Class 7 Geography Chapter 1, Environment, the child learns about the various Components of the Environment.

  • 7 class Geography Chapter 2 Inside Our Earth discusses the interior of the Earth.

  • Class 7 Geography Chapter 3, Our Changing Earth talks about the different natural phenomenon which takes place inside the Earth.

  • 7 class Geography Chapter 4, Air tells about the structure, composition of Atmosphere and weather and climate.

  • Class 7 Geography Chapter 5, Water, the child learns about the distribution of various water bodies and natural phenomenon linked.

  • 7 class Geography Chapter 6, the child also gets to know about Natural Vegetation and Wildlife.

  • Class 7 Geography Chapter 7, Human environment- settlements, transport, communication.

  • 7 class Geography Chapter 8, Human-Environment Interaction – the tropical and subtropical region is discussed in the chapter.

  • Class 7 Geography Chapter 9, provides a glimpse to the students about life in temperate grasslands.

  • Lastly, 7 class Geography Chapter 10, talks about Life in Deserts and explains different types of deserts.

7 CLASS CIVICS (Social and political life) -

The book starts by talking about the basic right of equality.

  • Class 7 Civics Chapter 1, On Equality, the child learns about their basic rights, responsibilities and the functioning of governmental bodies.

  • 7 class Civics Chapter 2 tells about the role of Government in health.

  • Class 7 Civics Chapter 3 explains the functioning of the State Government.

  • 7 class Civics Chapter 4 deals with the concept of Gender and shows how girls and boys are brought up or socialised differently.

  • Class 7 Civics Chapter 5 further develops ideas around gender inequalities in the world of work and describes women’s struggles for equality.

  • 7 class Civics Chapter 6 is about Understanding Media. Hence, the focus is on explaining the strong links between media and technology and the widespread effects of media.

  • Class 7 Civics Chapter 7 and 8 helps in understanding the working of the market, examining the chain of markets operates etc.

  • And lastly, 7 class Civics Chapter 9 talks about the Struggle for Equality of various groups.

The textbook contains class 7 SST all chapter which also includes various case studies, stories, classroom activities, data analysis and photographs which encourage students to learn and think more.

What is the course curriculum of Class 7 English main textbook and supplementary reader?

NCERT has prescribed two books to the students for the preparation of class 7 exams chapter wise.
The 7 class English book is -
Main textbook - Honeycomb
Supplementary reader - An Alien Hand

NCERT CLASS 7 ENGLISH MAIN TEXTBOOK HONEYCOMB (PROSE)

  • Class 7 English Chapter 1 “Three questions” is about three questions asked by the king. Also, the king promises a large sum of money in return to anyone who would answer the three questions.

  • 7 class English Chapter 2 “A Gift of Chappals” focuses on the simplicity of children’s behaviour. Further, it sees how they wish to explore so many aspects of this human world.

  • Class 7 English Chapter 3 “Gopal and the Hilsa Fish” is a moral based tale that provides a lesson. Also, it explains that nothing is impossible in this world once you learn to make smart decisions.

  • 7 class English Chapter 4 “The Ashes that made trees bloom” is a Japanese story that underscores the important values of humanity. They explore values of honesty and compassion.

  • Class 7 English Chapter 5 “Quality” is about german shoemakers, the Gessler brothers who died of starvation in the end.

  • 7 class English Chapter 6 “Expert Detectives” is about two siblings who are certainly worried about a stranger.

  • Class 7 English Chapter 7 “The Inventions of Vita - Wonk is based on an assumption that imagination plays a certain massive role in developing magical items.

  • 7 class English Chapter 8 “Fire: Friend and Foe” talks about the advantages and disadvantages of the invention of fire. Further, it explains the possible methods to prevent fire.

  • Class 7 English Chapter 9 “A bicycle in good repair” is a humorous tale. Here, the author's friend dismantles his bicycle while repairing it.

  • 7 class English Chapter 10 “The story of cricket” provides answers to the multiple questions related to the sport “Cricket”. Further, it compares it with other sports.

NCERT CLASS 7 ENGLISH MAIN TEXTBOOK “HONEYCOMB” (POEMS)

  • Class 7 English poem “The squirrel” by M.B Armstrong all about a description of a squirrel. Further, it explains how it moves, the food it likes, how it plays and so on.

  • 7 class English poem “The Rebel” by D.J. Enright is all about the character Rebel. Here, this Rebel does not follow the norms and conventions of society and is different.

  • Class 7 English poem “The Shed” by Frank Flynn is all about the fear of a child about a Shed present in the garden.

  • 7 class English poem “Chivvy” by Michael Rosen is all about how children are frequently told by grown-up kids. Moreover, it focuses on what to do and what not to.

  • Class 7 English poem “Trees” talks about the benefits that trees provide Individuals and society.

  • 7 class English poem ”Mystery of the talking fan” by Maude Rubin is all about a fan who is talking in some code languages with the poet.

  • Class 7 English poem “Dad and the cat and the tree” by Kit Wright is about the self-confidence of the dad. Also, how it failed in multiple attempts to bring the cat down from the tree.

  • 7 class English poem “Meadow surprises” Lois Brandt Phillips is about the beauty of the meadows filled with velvet grass.

  • Class 7 English poem “Garden Snake” by Murial L. Sonne is all about the advantages of having a Snake in the garden as it eats up the insects.

NCERT CLASS 7 ENGLISH SUPPLEMENTARY READER “AN ALIEN HAND”

  • Class 7 English Supplementary reader chapter 1 “The Tiny Teacher” by Gill Vaisey shares the sense of duties. consequently, it delivers the responsibilities to children to learn from sincerely.

  • 7 class English Supplementary reader chapter 2 “Bringing Up Kari” by Gopal Mukherji is about a five five-year-old elephant Kari. Also, it is about its upbringing by the narrator.

  • Class 7 English Supplementary reader chapter 3 “The Desert” describes a desert and break all the misconceptions. here, people have negative content in their minds related to a deserted place.

  • 7 class English Supplementary reader chapter 4 “The Cop and the Anthem” describes the themes of goals, dreams, hope. Here are some plans which play a major role in the success of our career and future.

  • Class 7 English Supplementary reader chapter 5 “Golu grows a Nose” is about a baby elephant whose name is Golu. He is curious about the things happening around him keep asking questions.

  • 7 class English Supplementary reader chapter 6 “I want something in a cage” is all about a shopkeeper who once encountered a strange person in his shop.

  • Class 7 English Supplementary reader chapter 7 “Chandni” is concerning a goat named Chandni. She refused to join a group of wild goats because she did not want to lose her freedom.

  • 7 class English Supplementary reader chapter 8 “The Bear Story” is about the love bear can shower on someone. Additionally, they too have a side which everyone loves even if they are wild.

  • Class 7 English Supplementary reader chapter 9 “A tiger in the house” by Ruskin Bond is regarding dealing with wild animals like a tiger with love and compassion.

  • 7 class English Supplementary reader chapter 10 “An Alien hand” is about the characters who live on the planet Mars under certain artificial conditions.

Summary of 7 class English all chapter is prepared deeply and analytically deeply and analytically with in-depth explanation and information.
